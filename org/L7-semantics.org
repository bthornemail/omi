* Modal Tautology
The key words "MUST", "MUST NOT", "REQUIRED", "SHALL", "SHALL
NOT", "SHOULD", "SHOULD NOT", "RECOMMENDED", "MAY", and
"OPTIONAL" in this layer specification are to be interpreted
as described in RFC 2119.

** S-P-O Definition (with RFC 2119 Modalities)

| Subject | Predicate         | Object       | MODALITY |
|---------+-------------------+--------------+----------|
| trace   | is_interpreted_as | structure    | MAY      |
| graph   | represents        | computation  | MAY      |
| state   | means             | meaning      | MAY      |

** Contract (Normative)
- Input: trace/state — MUST be valid trace/state from lower layers
- Output: semantic interpretation — MAY vary by context
- Responsibility: high-level meaning assignment — MUST NOT mutate trace or state
- Constraint: interpretation only, no mutation — MUST NOT alter system state

** OMI Mapping
- Category theory interpretation (admitted, not proven) — MAY be used for understanding
- CONS regions as graph semantics — MAY be identified in trace
- ASCII is operational, not semantic — MUST NOT assign semantic meaning to raw ASCII

** Implementation Notes
- This layer assigns meaning to the trace and state without altering them — MUST NOT write to trace or state
- It interprets the trace as a structure (e.g., a list, tree, or graph) — MAY produce different structures based on context
- The graph represents a computation (e.g., a reduction or transformation) — MAY be interpreted as different computations
- The state's meaning is derived from its role in the computation — MAY depend on observer context
- No mutation occurs at this layer; it is purely observational — MUST remain read-only with respect to trace and state
