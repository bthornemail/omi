* Timing Resolution Law

Timing periods are field-resolution partitions over one master replay surface,
not separate clock authorities.

#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
5040 = master blackboard resolution
360  = global state resolution
240  = public canvas-frame resolution
60   = private/local point-sweep resolution
8    = source/target byte-pair cadence
7    = incidence selector / consolidation law
#+END_EXAMPLE

The master period is:

#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
LCM(7, 60, 8, 240, 360) = 5040 = 7!
#+END_EXAMPLE

The =7 x 8= interaction resolves source-target relations into stable state.
One =5040= blackboard contains 90 complete =7 x 8= consolidation cycles.
